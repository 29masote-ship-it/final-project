Greek mythology isn't just about the figures, there are many sotries too! There were many reasons for these tales. They were to explain natural phenomena, explore human nature, define religious and solcial norms, and teach lessons. There were many greek tales, but a few are displayed down below.

Persephone and The Seasons:

As an introduction to the story, this story is meant to explain the seasons (aka why it is cold for 6 months). It involves Demeter, Demeter's daughter: Persephone, Hades and Zeus.

One day, while Persephone was picking flowers, Hades, the king of the underworld, kidnapped her and took her to the underworld. Demeter, the Goddess of harvest, fell into a deep greif. This grief caused the earth to become cold and dormant, like it would be in the winter. To bind her to his realm, Hades gave Persephone a bowl of pomegrante seeds to eat, which she only ate 6. Since she consumed food in the underworld, she was required to stay there. When Zeus saw what was happening on earth, he decided to negotiate a deal with Hades. The deal was that Persephone would return to her mother, Demeter, for half of the year. The other half of the year Persephone would stay with Hades. This is where the seasons formed. When persephone was with her mother, her mother was at ease, causing the weather to be nicer for that half of the year, thus causing summer and spring. This was also the time of the year for great harvest. During the 6 months that Persephone was with Hades, Demeter's grief would return, causing the weater to be cold and thus causing winter and autumn.

The Trojan War (how it began):

Although this story doesn't describe the whole Trojan war, It does describe how it started, as it did involve some of the gods and goddesses. It involes a dispute between Hera, Athena, and Aphrodite that was caused by Eris (basically goddess of chaos). Spoiler alert as this is not the true was the Trojan war started. It is a story that was made for the purpose of explaining why it started, but from history's prospective, it was most likely some conflicts between Troy and some Greek kingdoms.

One day, at the wedding between Peleus and Thetis, many gods were invited except for Eris (most likely for the fact she would ruin the vibe). Eris was not happy with this and enraged, she threw a golden apple into the wedding and on it was a note that said, "for the fairest". Hera, Aphrodite, and Athena all argued that it was for them, as each one of them saw only themselves as the fairest and the one most deserving of the apple. They all asked Zeus to say which was the fairest, and not willing to deal with the warth of any of them being upset, sent them down to Paris to be judged. Paris was a shepherd, but he was known for his honesty anf fairness. Zeus viewed him as a humble judge for this reason and ended up sending the 3 goddesses down to him. Hera, Athena and Aphrodite all went down there and told him to point at the fairest goddess. He hesitated, and the goddesses took this as an oppritunity to each offer up a bribe for him to choose them. Hera offered him power. Athena offered him widsom. Aphrodite offered him the most beautiful girl in the world. Paris ended up choosing Aphrodite, and Aphrodite promised him the love of Helen, aka the Queen of Sparta.

This may seem like no reason to start a war, but there was more than just the fact that now Helen was supposed to be inlove with Paris.

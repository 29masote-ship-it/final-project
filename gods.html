There were many Greek Mythology features. Over 3,000 if you include lesser deities. But there are 12 main gods, or Olympians, not including Hades, Persephone, and Hecate, which are seen as just deities evem though they are a big topic of Greek Myth. Each god plays a diffrent role in tales and the overall enviornment on Mount Olympus, and in Greece aswell (Atleast according to myth). Every god also has a diffrent thing they are in charge of and control. These gods are a big part in Ancient Greek stories, and these stories said alot about their personalities. A big thing that they all have in common is that they usually act out of anger, or without thinking. Here is some more in depth descriptions of each of the gods.

Zeus

Zeus was very important on Mount Olympus. He was the god of the sky, weather, and mainly thunder. His main symbol is a thunderbolt (Which was his main weapon). He was seen as a king, or a ruler of Mount Olympus. He was also known to control the law and fate in Ancient Greece and in Mount Olympus, and punish any wrongdoing. Zeus was a son of the titans Cronus and Rhea. The personality of Zues is known to be authoritive(as he was the the leader of Olympus), but also impulsive and unfaithful. He was also short tempered and quite vengful, sometimes even controlling. His rise to power is a complicated story. To sum it up, he had to avoid being eaten by his father and was hid in a crete as an infant. He later returned and forced his father, Cronus, to disgorge his siblings. from then on he was seen as a leader. Zeus was married to Hera, but has many affairs behind Hera's back. In result of these affairs, he has had many children. This is a reason for why there are so many demi-gods and deities when it comes to Greeck Mythology. His most famous sanctuary (aka cult site) is Olympia.

Poseidon

Poseiden is most famously known for being the god of the sea, but he is also god of earthquakes and horses. His main symbol is his trident (a three pronged spear), which was his main weapon. He was also known as the ruler of the sea and the "Earth Shaker". He was a son of the titans Cronus and Rhea. His personality was moody, and unpredictable, which reflect the nature of the sea. He is also a little short tempered. Poseidon was married to Amphitrite, and served as a queen of the sea. Amphitrite was a sea nymph. The was they met was that Poseidon saw her dancing, but when he tried to approch her, she hid. Poseidon later sent a dolphin to find her and convince her to marry him. They ended up having a small family together. hus most famous sanctuary is the temple of Poseidon, at Cape Sounion.

Athena

Athena is one of the strongest and toughest gods. She is most known for being the goddess of wisdom, but she is also the goddess of crafts and strategic warfare. Her main symbol is an owl, and this was for the wisdom and owl has is like the wisdom she has. Her personality is mature and disciplined, sometimes even ruthless. She is also known to be very strong. There are multiple occasions of her displaying her strength, many of them even being her beating up and humiliating Ares. Athena is the daughter of Zeus, but her birth happened in a strange way. She was born out of Zeus's forehead after Zeus had swallowed her mother. She was born in full armor and was fully grown. She remained very loyal to Zeus. Athena has never fallen in love, as she has never felt that way about anyone. Athena has a whole town for herself, Athens. Her most famous sanctuary, the Parthenon, is located in Athens.

Ares

Ares was known as thr god of war and violence. His main symbol was a sword, as he was the god of war. His diffrence from Athena (Ares: god of war, Athena: god of stragistic warfare) is that Ares was more just violence, rage, and wanting to fight. His personality represents this as he was chaotic, violent, brutal, and impulsive. Why he is like this is to represent the dirty aspects of war, as in the blood, and violence. Basically, he was used in myths to represent the negative consequences of war and violence He was even cassociated with the Thracians, as they were often viewed as barbaric people. Ares is the son of Zeus and Hera. But he was often scorned by his parents. In fact, he was mostly disliked by other gods, and even mortals, because of the fact that he was basically the god of rage and violence. One godess did end up liking him, Aphrodite. They did have a few moments together, but were never considered married. Since he was quite disliked, there was never a very famous santuary for Ares. But one of his sanctuaries was located in the Athenian Agora.

Aphrodite

Aphrodite is the godess of love and beauty. Her main symbols are doves and roses. Aphrodite's personality was passonite and loving, but also manipulative and crafty. She also gets very jealous if her beauty is challenged. Aphrodite is most famously known for how beautiful she was considered to be. She is also known for how unfaithful she was. Aphrodite was married to Hephaestus through an arranged marrige that Zeus had arranged. It was known as the worst match in Olympus, as Hephaestus was known as awkward. Aphrodite was quite unfaithful to Hephaestus. The god she really loved was Ares. Aphrodite is the daughter of Zeus and Dione. She was also the main cause of the Trojan war because she had promised Helen to Paris after Paris said that she was the most beautiful godess. Aphrodite is also known as the mother of Eros, or in other words Cupid. Her most famous sanctuary is on cyprus, called The Sanctuary of Aphrodite.

Hera

Hera was a big part of Olympus, as she was basically the queen of gods. She is the goddess of marrige, women, and childbrith. Her main symbols are a peacock and a pomegrante. She was known of the queen of gods because she was married to Zeus. Her personality is known to be very jealous and vengful, often getting relentless when interacting with any of Zeus's offspring Also, despite being married to a very unfaithful partner, she was very loyal to him. Hera is the daughter of the titans Kornus and Rhea. Her most famous sanctuary was the Heraion of Samos.

Demeter

Demeter was the goddess of harvest and argiculture. Her main symbol was wheat ears. Her personality was known to be mother like and nurturing. She was the daughter of Cronus and Rhea. Her lover was Iasion, but she also had a daughter with Zeus, Persephone. Demeter is the main reason for the seasons, as her daughter, Persephone, was abducted by hades.
